What do my results mean?

When asked to switch between two distinct tasks, people take longer to respond than when they are able to focus on a single task entirely. This "switch cost" is thought to reflect the fact that your brain must temporarily reorganize to respond correctly.
Switching ability is thought to be a critical executive function that allows us to pay attention and deal with multiple things at once. The fact that we exhibit switch costs when doing more than one thing at a time is a reason why driving and doing other activites (such as texting) can be dangerous.

Why do we measure executive functions?

Executive functions help us all navigate the complex world we live in. They help us multi-task, plan for the future, stay on task, complete goals, and remembering to remember ("I need to remember to go to the grocery store after work"). Executive functions are related to many health outcomes and mental health outcomes, such as decline in neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's or Parkinsons.
